The Grand Slam Offer Builder

Build an offer so good people feel stupid saying no — based on Alex Hormozi's $100M Offers

Step 1

Who is this offer for?

Every great offer starts with a starving crowd. Nail down the person and the problem before anything else.

"The goal is to charge as much money for your products or services as humanly possible... make them so good people feel stupid saying no."
Step 2

The Value Equation

Value is the fulcrum of your offer. Increase the top, decrease the bottom.

Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
⬆ Dream Outcome5
How big & desirable is the result? Higher = more valuable.
⬆ Perceived Likelihood of Achievement5
How certain are they it'll work FOR THEM? Proof, guarantees, testimonials raise this.
⬇ Time Delay5
How long until they see results? Lower = more valuable. Deliver fast wins early.
⬇ Effort & Sacrifice5
How much work/pain for them? Lower = more valuable. Do it for them where possible.

Relative Value Score
Step 3

List Every Problem

Between your customer and their dream outcome sits a wall of problems. List them all — every obstacle, fear, and hurdle. Each one becomes a solution you can sell or stack.

"For every problem there is a solution... to sell a solution, we must know the problem."
Step 4

Turn Problems into Solutions

Flip each problem into the thing that solves it. Phrase solutions as "How to..." outcomes. These are the building blocks of your offer stack.

Step 5

Trim & Stack the Offer

You can't include everything. Keep the solutions with high value to the customer and low cost to you. Trim the rest. What survives becomes your Grand Slam stack.

"The goal is to find offers with high value and low cost to fulfill — attractive to customers, cheap for you."

Load your solutions and rate each. The app highlights your keepers.

Step 6

Price with Confidence

Charge premium prices. High prices force high value and attract better clients. Add up what the stack is worth, then set your price far below that stacked value.

"Charge the most you possibly can... higher prices create higher perceived value — and higher client results."
—
Value-to-Price Ratio (aim for 10× or more)
Step 7

Enhance with Bonuses

A single offer at $1,000 is weaker than a core offer plus stacked bonuses that total the same. Bonuses solve the next problem, address objections, and make the deal feel overwhelming.

"The main offer + bonuses that each independently solve a problem >> one lumped offer."
Step 8

Reverse the Risk (Guarantee)

A strong guarantee removes the fear of buying. Pick a type and write yours. Boldness sells — a specific, gutsy guarantee out-converts a vague one.

"A guarantee is a formal assurance... reversing risk is one of the most powerful ways to increase conversion."
Step 9

Scarcity & Urgency

Value is tied to supply and demand. Scarcity (limited quantity) and urgency (limited time) push people to act now instead of "later" — which usually means never.

"Scarcity is a function of quantity. Urgency is a function of time."
